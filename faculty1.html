Research Interests

Professor Davidson's research focuses on Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and its impact on the design and usability of interactive systems. His work delves into understanding user experience, accessibility, and cognitive aspects of digital interfaces.

His recent projects have included exploring the role of machine learning in personalizing user experiences and studying the effects of augmented reality on learning environments. He is passionate about creating inclusive technologies that cater to a diverse range of users, particularly those with disabilities.

Professor Davidson collaborates with other researchers in the fields of psychology and computer science to develop systems that are both effective and intuitive, aiming to bridge the gap between human needs and technological innovation.
